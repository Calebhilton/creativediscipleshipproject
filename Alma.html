Alma 30

The story of Korihor, an anti-Christ who preaches against Christ and God's commandments, but is struck dumb by God's power and ultimately dies in disgrace.

Alma 31

Alma leads a missionary effort to reclaim the Zoramites, who have fallen into pride and false worship, teaching them to humble themselves and pray with real intent.

Alma 32

Faith begins as a small desire, like a seed, and if nurtured through humility and obedience, it will grow into a strong and perfect knowledge of truth.

Alma 33

The words of ancient prophets like Zenos, Zenock, and Moses testify that people should pray and exercise faith in the Son of God to receive His mercy and salvation.

Alma 34

Amulek testifies of Jesus Christ’s atoning sacrifice, urges the people to repent without delay, and emphasizes that this life is the time to prepare to meet God.

Alma 35

The Zoramites who believed in Alma’s teachings were cast out, found refuge with the people of Ammon, and how the anger of the remaining Zoramites led to increasing tensions and war preparations with the Lamanites.

Alma 36-42

Alma counsels his sons—teaching them about his own conversion, the power of the scriptures, the importance of faith, repentance, chastity, life after death, resurrection, and how God’s justice and mercy work together through the Atonement of Jesus Christ.

Alma 43-44

The beginning of war between the Nephites and Lamanites, where Captain Moroni leads the Nephites in defending their freedom and faith, ultimately defeating the Lamanites and offering them peace if they will surrender.

Alma 45-51

Captain Moroni’s efforts to defend the Nephites against increasing Lamanite attacks, including fortifying cities, creating the title of liberty, facing internal traitors like Amalickiah, and dealing with pride and contention among the Nephites.

Alma 52-62

The ongoing Nephite-Lamanite war, highlighting Captain Moroni’s and Helaman’s courage, the faith and miracles of the 2,000 stripling warriors, and the eventual Nephite victories despite internal challenges and hardships.

Alma 63

Hagoth, a curious Nephite, builds ships and leads many people to explore lands across the sea, while Helaman continues to keep the sacred records and the Nephites enjoy a brief period of peace.
