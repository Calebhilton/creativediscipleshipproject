Helaman 1

Contention and pride among the Nephites over the judgment seat, leading to the secret combinations of Kishkumen and the Lamanite invasion led by Coriantumr, who captures Zarahemla but is eventually defeated and killed.

Helaman 2

Helaman becomes chief judge and survives an assassination attempt by Kishkumen, whose secret band is later exposed and destroyed by Helaman's servant.

Helaman 3

A time of peace and prosperity among the Nephites, many of whom migrate northward, while pride begins to enter the Church, but those who remain humble and faithful become strong through the word of God.

Helaman 4

The Nephites, because of their pride and wickedness, lose many of their lands to the Lamanites, but eventually humble themselves and begin to reclaim their territory with the Lord’s help.

Helaman 5

Nephi and Lehi preach repentance with great power, are imprisoned by Lamanites, but through their faith, a miraculous event with fire and the voice of God converts many Lamanites to Christ.

Helaman 6

A time of peace and prosperity as many Lamanites are converted to the gospel, while the Nephites begin to fall into wickedness and secret combinations led by the Gadianton robbers.

Helaman 7

Nephi, saddened by the wickedness of the Nephites, prays on his tower and boldly calls the people to repent, warning them of destruction if they continue in sin.

Helaman 8

Nephi continues to preach repentance, testifies of prophets like Moses, and prophesies the murder of the chief judge to prove his divine power and the truth of his words.

Helaman 9

Nephi’s prophecy of the chief judge’s murder comes true, leading some people to believe he is a prophet, while others harden their hearts and continue to resist his message.

Helaman 10

Nephi is blessed by God with great power to command according to God’s will, and he faithfully continues preaching repentance to the people despite their wickedness.

Helaman 11

Nephi prays for a famine to humble the Nephites and stop their wars, and after they repent, the Lord ends the famine, but the people soon return to wickedness and the Gadianton robbers grow in strength again.

Helaman 12

Teaches that humans are often prideful, foolish, and quick to forget God, but the Lord is merciful, all-powerful, and will bless those who repent and follow Him.

Helaman 13

Samuel the Lamanite stands on the city wall and boldly warns the wicked Nephites of their coming destruction if they do not repent of their pride, riches, and rebellion against God.

Helaman 14

Samuel the Lamanite prophesies of the birth and death of Jesus Christ, giving signs of His coming, including a day and a night and a day with no darkness at His birth, and three days of darkness at His death.

Helaman 15

Samuel the Lamanite praises the faithfulness of the Lamanites, warning the Nephites that unless they repent, they will lose their blessings and the Lord will remember the Lamanites because of their righteousness.

Helaman 16

Samuel the Lamanite continues to preach repentance, warning of signs of Christ’s birth and death, while many believe his words but others reject and try to kill him.
