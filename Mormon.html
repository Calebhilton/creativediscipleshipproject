Mormon 1

Mormon chapter 1 shows the beginning of Mormon’s story as a young boy chosen by Ammaron to keep the sacred records during a time of great wickedness and war.

Mormon 2

Mormon leading the Nephite armies in devastating battles while sorrowing over their wickedness, as they refuse to repent despite their suffering and loss.

Mormon 3

Mormon leading the Nephites with hope for their repentance, but after their rejection of God, he refuses to lead them to victory, knowing their pride will lead to destruction.

Mormon 4

The tragic destruction of the Nephite people as pride, vengeance, and terrible warfare lead to great suffering, loss, and wickedness on both sides.

Mormon 5

Mormon sorrowfully leading his wicked people in their final battles, knowing their destruction is near, and writing to future generations as a warning and testimony of Christ.

Mormon 6

The tragic final battle at Cumorah, where the Nephite nation is destroyed, and Mormon mourns the loss of his people with great sorrow and lamentation.

Mormon 7

Mormon’s final message, pleading with the Lamanites to believe in Christ, repent, be baptized, and accept the truth found in the Book of Mormon and the Bible.

Mormon 8

Moroni alone, finishing his father's record after the destruction of the Nephites, and warning future readers about pride, wickedness, and rejecting God's word.

Mormon 9

Moroni boldly testifying that God is a God of miracles, urging future readers to have faith, repent, and believe in Christ’s power to save and transform lives.
